Type: tabula Iliaca
Origin: Roman
Category: relief
Material: marble
Dating: -27 – 14
Description
Fragment. In front of the seated Agamemnon the bearded Chryses kneeling, touching the knees of the king with outstretched arms in a beseeching manner. On the right a carriage filled with goods, containing the ransom for Chryseis. Another scene showing Chryses approaching the temple of Apollon Smintheus with raised right arm. To the right of the temple Apollon drawing arrows. The two figures in zone 3 are named by inscriptions (Achilleus and Agamemnon). In another scene Hektor follows Nestor, both are on chariots. Between them the duel between Paris and Aias. Pandaros depicted in two friezes, in the fourth frieze with Athena and Diomedes. Thetis with the shield of Achilleus which is decorated with a zodiac and the earth.
